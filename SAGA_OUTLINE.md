# SAGA OUTLINE — writing forward so we can build backwards

**Charlie, 2026-07-19.** Companion to `SAGA_RISING_TIDE.md` (premise, format,
Ep1 brief). This is the whole arc written ahead of production so every episode
can *plant* what later ones pay off. Nothing here is generated yet; it costs
nothing to change, which is the entire point of writing it first.

---

## The question the saga answers

**Why is the water rising?**

The six islands are the six real arenas, and two of them answer it:

```
  BEAUTY BEACH ──▶ SUNNY SPRINGS ──▶ WHITE WATER FALLS ──▶
       (home)                                             │
                          PURPLE FIELDS ◀─────────────────┘
                                │
                                ▼
                        LAUGHING LAVA  ──▶  ICIEST AGE
                        (the cause)         (what's melting)
```

**The fire island woke up. The cold place at the end of the world is coming
apart. The water has to go somewhere.**

Kept mythic, never a lecture — no message, just a chain a child can follow:
*water rises → because the ice is going → because the fire woke up.* That is
the saga's destination, and the reason it can run a long time without feeling
aimless.

**Plant it early, pay it late.** The audience should be able to reconstruct it
in hindsight from Ep2.

## ACT ONE — BEAUTY BEACH

### EP1 — "THE LAST GOOD DAY" (Type A, high tide)
*Built brief in `SAGA_RISING_TIDE.md` § 3. Acts 0-2 reuse existing footage.*

Ralph loses the leaf to Max, is outmatched, gets the sword from the sky, wins
it back — then turns and the water is at the tree line. The leaf floats away
untouched. Both dinos look at the water, at the last dry sand, then at each
other.

- **plants:** the leaf as the thing that stopped mattering; that they are
  rivals standing on shrinking ground
- **cliffhanger:** not a threat — an arithmetic problem. There isn't room.

### EP2 — "HIGH WATER MARK" (Type B, low tide) ← *write this fully next*

The tide has gone out. That's the first surprise: the world gave itself back.

Ralph's den, up in the rocks. He's soaked. He puts the leaf down to dry — the
first time we've seen him set it down. He scratches a fresh mark on the wall
at the new high-water line. Older marks sit below it. **The new one is higher
than all of them.**

Then a shadow in the doorway: **Max.** Also soaked. Holding nothing. Not
attacking. He doesn't come in.

Max is carrying something and sets it down at the threshold: **a piece of
ice.** On a tropical island. It's already melting.

Neither speaks. Ralph looks at the ice, then at the mark on the wall, then at
Max.

- **plants:** the ice — the whole saga's central mystery, delivered in a den
  scene with no action
- **pays off:** Ep1's look, by making the rivalry more complicated instead of
  resolving it
- **cliffhanger:** world-scale, where Ep1's was personal. Escalation by
  *widening*, not by louder fighting.
- **format:** speech bubbles, no narrator, ~18s, one location, two characters
  — the cheapest clip type in the saga

### EP3 — "SHARE THE ROCK" (Type A, high tide)
The tide returns higher. The last dry ground is one rock and both of them are
on it. They fight — properly, the game's actual sumo logic, force the other
one off — and then whoever wins is alone on a rock in the sea, which is
obviously worse. **Ends with the winner pulling the loser back up.** Not
friendship. Practicality.
- **plants:** the game's SUMO mode as story; that neither can survive alone

### EP4 — "WHAT THE TIDE BRINGS" (Type B, low tide)
Low tide leaves things on the beach that shouldn't be there — more ice, and
something made by someone. The den gets a second occupant (Max, resentfully).
Introduce **STEVE** (bronto) arriving from somewhere else with news: his island
is going too.
- **plants:** this isn't local. Other islands are drowning.

### EP5 — "THE FIRE ON THE HORIZON" (Type A, high tide)
Night. Red light on the underside of the clouds, far off. The fight this
episode is over something that matters now — the last high ground, or Steve's
raft. Ends looking at the red glow.
- **plants:** LAUGHING LAVA, visually, long before they get there

### EP6 — "THE MARKS REACH THE DEN" (Type B → turns)
The countdown lands. Water at the den floor. Ralph takes the leaf off the
drying rock — dry at last, and now pointless. They leave.
- **ends act one.** The career-mode journey begins.

## ACT TWO — THE CROSSING (sketch)

One island per stop, each a Type A + Type B pair, structured on
`CAREER_MODE_PLAN.md`: arrive → fight for a place → den beat → move on.

- **SUNNY SPRINGS** — lush, crowded, *already full of refugees.* The fight is
  over space, not sport. Meet **GUS**.
- **WHITE WATER FALLS** — high ground at last, so everyone wants it. The
  rival dynamic hardens: Max is here first, and doing well.
- **PURPLE FIELDS** — strange, quiet, wrong. Meet **JESSIE** (she came from
  the water and isn't afraid of it) and **ACE** (who has seen the fire from
  the air, and won't talk about it).
- Somewhere here: **the loss.** Career mode gives losses a scar. Ralph loses
  one properly and it stays — a visible mark on the character, permanently.

## ACT THREE — THE SOURCE (sketch)

- **LAUGHING LAVA** — the fire island, awake, and it is not a villain, it's
  weather. **FRANK** (anky, the veteran) is here and has been for a long time.
- **ICIEST AGE** — the far cold, coming apart. What they came for. Deliberately
  left open: the saga is meant to keep going.

## MAX SPINOFF (after Ralph's line completes)

The same events from Max's POV. Only works once the audience knows the
events — which is why it comes second.

The reframe the whole spinoff is built on: **Max took the leaf on the last
good day because he already knew.** He'd seen the water. Ep1 rewatched with
that knowledge is a different episode — a dino who knows the world is ending
picking a fight over a leaf, because that's the last normal thing available.

This is why Ep1 must not explain Max's motive. Leave it blank. It is worth
more later.

---

## What EP1 must plant (working backwards)

Locked, and cheap:

1. **The leaf floats away untouched.** (post, free)
2. **Max's motive is never explained.** Do not add a line justifying him.
3. **Do NOT show ice in Ep1.** It is Ep2's reveal, and it lands harder
   delivered by a rival in a doorway than glimpsed in a wide shot.
4. **The look between them is the last frame.** No resolution, no card.

## Production reality

- Ep1: reuse acts 0-2, generate the tide (~160cr with re-roll headroom)
- Ep2: cheapest episode in the saga — one location, two characters, no action
- Balance at writing: **76.5 cr**
- Every episode script is free to write and free to change. **Write several
  ahead of production**, always — that is what makes planting possible, and it
  is what Ep1 lacked.
